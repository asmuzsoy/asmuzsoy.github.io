ANA SOFIA UZSOY

I am an Astronomy PhD student at Harvard working at the intersection of astrophysics, statistics, and machine learning to help us harness the power of big data to better explore and understand the universe.

ABOUT ME

I am a second-year PhD student in the Astronomy department at Harvard working with Prof. Doug Finkbeiner. My current research involves developing statistical technqiues for spectral component separation and applying them to Lyman-Alpha Emitter galaxies using the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI). My research interests broadly involve astrostatistics and applications of statistical & machine learning for observational cosmology.

In 2022, I graduated from the University of Cambridge as a Churchill Scholar with an M.Phil in Machine Learning and Machine Intelligence, with the thesis "Scalable Bayesian Inference for Probabilistic Spectrotemporal Models of Type Ia Supernovae".

In 2021, I graduated from NC State University as a Park Scholar, with a double major in physics and computer science and minors in math and music. While there, I was awarded a Goldwater Scholarship and interned at NASA and Google.

In my work, I strive to be equal parts astrophysicist and data scientist.
