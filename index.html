About Me

About Me

I am a fourth-year PhD candidate and NSF Graduate Research Fellow in the Astronomy department at Harvard, working with Prof. Doug Finkbeiner.

My research interests include galaxy spectra, Lyman-Alpha Emitters, galaxy-environment interactions, and applications of statistical & machine learning for extragalactic astrophysics.

In 2022, I graduated from the University of Cambridge as a Churchill Scholar with an M.Phil in Machine Learning and Machine Intelligence, where my thesis focused on variational inference applications to Type Ia supernova light curve fitting.

In my work, I strive to be equal parts astrophysicist and data scientist.
